Understanding Aerospace Digital Twins

An Aerospace Digital Twin is a continuously updated software replica of a physical aircraft or jet engine. By pairing live 100 Hz sensor feeds with physics-informed neural networks (PINNs), operators can predict structural fatigue and calculate Remaining Useful Life (RUL) long before failure occurs.

How Gidex AeroAI Explorer Works

Gidex AeroAI Explorer is an interactive flight telemetry laboratory designed to demonstrate how artificial intelligence replaces legacy single-sensor alert systems in modern aviation.

1. Predictive Engine Diagnostics

Adjust 7 thermodynamic sliders (TIT Temperature, Vibration, Fuel Pressure, Lube Oil Debris). Observe how neural network anomaly scoring predicts Remaining Useful Life (RUL) before hardware failure occurs.

2. Airframe Digital Twin Strain

Simulate Mach airspeed and wing flex angle to observe real-time structural stress (MPa) and skin friction drag.

3. DRL Trajectory Optimization

Simulate Deep Reinforcement Learning agents calculating optimal detour curves around severe convective weather cells.
